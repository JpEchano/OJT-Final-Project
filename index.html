ABOUT US

The name of our organization is Supreme Student Council. The SSC is a group of elected students working together with the supervision of the club adviser within the framework of a constitution or by-laws to provide a means for student expression and assistance in school activities, give opportunities for student to experience leadership and encourage student to collaborate with the faculty and the community.

ACCOMPLISTMENTS

First Meeting

Being a recognized organization within the institution entails our responsibility to address crucial matters through formal meetings. The initial gathering of the Supreme Student Council took place on September 17, 2023, commencing precisely at 1:00 PM. Key agenda items included preparing for the leadership and induction camp, revising the current constitution and by-laws, as well as suggesting activities for the academic year 2023-2024. The session concluded at 4:00 PM and was well-attended by all council members.

World Teachers' Day

World Teachers' Day is a momentous occasion that honors the dedication and invaluable contributions of educators worldwide. In the academic year 2023, the Supreme Student Council and the Student Coordinating Body of La Consolacion College of Daet took the initiative to organize a series of meaningful activities to celebrate this significant event. The day commenced at 8:00 in the morning with a vibrant opening program, skillfully led by Mr. Jan Patrick A. Echano and Ms. Dorothy N. Santiago, esteemed officers of the Supreme Student Council, as the Masters of the Ceremony. The program began with a solemn Doxology presented by representatives of the Supreme Student Council, followed by the uplifting rendition of the National Anthem conducted by Ms. Aira Villan. The atmosphere brimmed with anticipation as attendees awaited the Welcoming Remarks delivered by Sr. Angelita J. Sagun, OSA, the esteemed school president. Subsequently, Mrs. Maria Eloisa Belarma, the Family Council President, and Mrs. Cecil Ibasco, the Family Council Vice President, shared their heartfelt messages, setting the tone for a day filled with appreciation and gratitude towards our beloved teachers. Consequenlty, set of games was then facilitated by the Supreme Student Government, namely: Guess the Word, Evolution of Hero, Touch and Caress, Sing/Guess the Next Lyrics, Left and Right, and Pour the Water.

Leadership Camp

The leadership camp and induction ceremony held at Sitio Mat-i Vinzons, Camarines Norte, embodied the theme "Flames of Leadership: Ignite, Inspire, and Protect" on October 7-8, 2023. The event was attended by College Students, SSC Officers, NSTP Officers and Head, School Head, Teaching Staff, and BFP Officers. The camp kicked off with an energetic ice-breaking activity that brought participants together, setting the tone for collaboration and camaraderie. Throughout the weekend, workshops and seminars were conducted focusing on various aspects of leadership, including communication skills, problem-solving, and decision-making. One of the highlights was a team-building exercise where participants had to navigate through a series of challenges that tested their leadership abilities, teamwork, and adaptability. This activity fostered a sense of unity and taught valuable lessons on cooperation and effective leadership strategies. The induction ceremony, held on the final day, was a solemn yet celebratory affair. Newly elected officers were sworn in, pledging their commitment to serve with integrity and dedication. Inspirational speeches to encouraged the newly inducted leaders to embrace their roles with passion and purpose. As the flames flickered and danced, so did the aspirations of these young leaders, ready to ignite positive change, inspire others, and protect the values they hold dear. Overall, the Leadership Camp and Induction Ceremony was a resounding success, leaving a lasting impact on all participants and setting the stage for a year filled with growth, empowerment, and impactful leadership. In fostering a culture of camaraderie and leadership development, La Consolacion College of Daet, Inc. held its annual Supreme Student Council Election for the school year 2023-2024. This activity not only promotes unity but also provides an avenue for students to nurture their talents, skills, and capabilities in a collaborative environment. The newly elected set of officers for the Supreme Student Council represents a diverse group of individuals from the college department, each bringing unique perspectives and strengths to the council.

Clean-up Drive

The Supreme Student Council of LCC-D led a transformative clean-up drive at Sitio Mantabog on November 4, 2023. Alongside Sr. Angelita J. Sagun, Sr. Aine Rosaldes, and adviser Mr. Hector A. Alegre, students actively participated in restoring the environment. Gathering at the site, volunteers worked tirelessly to clear pathways and riverbanks, fostering unity and responsibility. Mr. Alegre's guidance fueled their dedication, resulting in a tangible impact on the community's cleanliness and environmental consciousness. This initiative not only cleaned up physical debris but also instilled a lasting commitment to environmental stewardship and community service among participants, leaving a positive mark on both the volunteers and the Sitio Mantabog community.

Mobile Legends Tournament

As the annual Foundation Celebration at La Consolacion College of Daet, Inc. approaches, our advisor, Sir Hector Alegre, has entrusted us with the responsibility of managing the Mobile Legends Tournament. Given the high demand for this event among the students, the organizers have decided to accommodate their request. During our previous Mobile Legends Tournament, we encountered issues with the internet connection. To address these problems for our upcoming tournament, we explored several solutions, including assigning separate internet connections to each team to reduce bandwidth competition. We conducted trial matches, or "scrims," in collaboration with IT Catalyst and the CCS faculty to assess our options. After extensive testing, we decided to deploy a single, more powerful router capable of supporting all players simultaneously. To ensure a stable and exclusive network for the competitors, we will also frequently change the Wi-Fi password during the tournament, allowing only the players in the current match to connect. Additionally, as each competition or tournament requires an advisor, Sir Ian Ceasar Abarca has been assigned to assist and supervise us during both the planning stages and the actual event. On the second day of the Foundation Celebration, we began our duties by conducting an orientation for the players. During this session, we explained the rules and regulations of the tournament and outlined how the brackets would be formed. As the tournament progressed, we witnessed the players' dedication and passion for the game. The competition intensified but remained friendly, with all participants upholding the spirit of sportsmanship throughout the event.

Concert for a Cause

After successfully facilitating the Mobile Legends Tournament and enjoying the overall event, we moved on to prepare for the Concert for a Cause. We were tasked with cleaning and setting up some parts of the venue for the band and the audience. Following the intense competitions from the sports tournaments and LITMUSDA, it's now time to come together and enjoy the performances of promising bands. This year's main attraction at the Concert for a Cause is SUGARCANE, a six-piece folk-pop band from Manila. Initially known for their cover songs, SUGARCANE has evolved, developing a unique sound that has made them a standout for their distinctive music and heartfelt storytelling. Their compositions, rooted in folk-pop, combine sweet melodies with meaningful lyrics that resonate with listeners, taking them on an emotional journey. Now part of Warner Music Philippines' roster of talented artists, SUGARCANE is looking to push their creative boundaries and continue producing music that not only showcases their artistry but also inspires and connects with people globally.
